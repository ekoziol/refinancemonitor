## Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

### Refinance Candidate Criteria
A homeowner is a potential refinance candidate if:
1. Current rate is 50+ basis points above available rates
2. Sufficient equity (typically 20%+)
3. Adequate credit score (typically 680+)
4. Breakeven timeline < remaining loan term
5. No prepayment penalties

### US SAM Analysis

**In a falling rate environment:**
- 30-40% of mortgaged homeowners could benefit from refinancing
- ~15-20 million potential refinance candidates

**In a stable/rising rate environment:**
- 10-15% could benefit (cash-out refis, term changes, ARM conversions)
- ~5-7 million potential candidates

### International SAM
- UK: 3-5M homeowners (shorter fixed terms mean more frequent refinancing)
- Canada: 2-4M homeowners (5-year terms standard)
- Australia/NZ: 2-3M homeowners
- **Total International SAM**: 10-15 million homeowners

### Combined SAM
- **Total SAM**: 25-40 million potential customers globally
- **Monetizable market** (willing to pay for alert services): $500M-2B annually

## Market Dynamics

### Rate Sensitivity
The refinancing market is highly cyclical and directly correlated with interest rate movements:

- **Rate drop of 50+ bps**: Triggers significant refinance wave
- **Rate drop of 100+ bps**: Massive refinance boom (2020-2021 example)
- **Rising rate environment**: Market contracts but opportunities remain for specific segments

### Timing Factors
1. **Fed policy cycles**: Rate cuts typically trigger 6-18 month refinance windows
2. **Seasonality**: Spring/summer see peak home purchase activity; refinancing peaks lag
3. **Economic uncertainty**: Recessions often prompt rate cuts and refinance opportunities

### Regulatory Considerations
- US: RESPA, TILA disclosure requirements
- UK: FCA mortgage conduct rules
- Canada: Provincial mortgage broker licensing
- **Opportunity**: Alert services are NOT mortgage advice, lower regulatory burden

### Indirect Competitors
- **Mortgage brokers**: Personal relationships but not scalable
- **Bank relationship managers**: Limited to bank's products
- **Financial advisors**: Low priority item, not automated
- **DIY spreadsheets**: Requires constant manual monitoring

### Why Competitors Are Vulnerable

1. **No "set and forget" automation**
   - All competitors require users to actively check
   - Refi Alert monitors continuously and alerts only when breakeven is achieved

2. **Conflict of interest**
   - Lead gen sites monetize by selling user data
   - Lenders push their own products
   - Refi Alert is user-aligned (subscription + optimal refinance)

3. **No breakeven intelligence**
   - Competitors show rates but don't calculate personalized breakeven
   - Our efficient frontier algorithm is unique IP

### Competitive Moat

**Technical Moat:**
- Efficient frontier algorithm calculates optimal refinance timing
- Breakeven analysis considers closing costs, remaining term, rate differential
- Multi-factor alert triggers (rate threshold, equity, credit score requirements)

**Data Moat:**
- Aggregated rate data from multiple sources
- User mortgage profiles enable better recommendations over time
- Market timing intelligence from aggregate behavior

## Customer Segments

### Primary Segments

#### 1. Rate Optimizers (40% of TAM)
- **Profile**: Financially savvy, actively tracking rates
- **Behavior**: Check rates weekly/monthly, have saved thousands through refinancing before
- **Pain point**: Time-consuming to monitor, might miss optimal window
- **Value prop**: Never miss an opportunity, automated monitoring
- **Willingness to pay**: High ($10-20/month)

#### 2. Life Event Refinancers (30% of TAM)
- **Profile**: Divorce, inheritance, job change, moving
- **Behavior**: Need to restructure mortgage due to life change
- **Pain point**: Don't know where to start, time-sensitive situation
- **Value prop**: Understand if/when refinancing makes sense for their situation
- **Willingness to pay**: Medium ($5-10/month or one-time)

#### 3. Cash-Out Refinancers (20% of TAM)
- **Profile**: Have equity, need cash for home improvement, debt consolidation, investment
- **Behavior**: Looking to tap equity without selling
- **Pain point**: Don't know optimal timing or rate to pull trigger
- **Value prop**: Alert when cash-out refi becomes cost-effective
- **Willingness to pay**: Medium ($5-10/month)

#### 4. Property Investors (10% of TAM)
- **Profile**: Multiple properties, sophisticated, ROI-focused
- **Behavior**: Actively manage portfolio, always optimizing
- **Pain point**: Tracking multiple mortgages across properties is complex
- **Value prop**: Portfolio-wide monitoring, aggregate savings analysis
- **Willingness to pay**: Very High ($30-50/month for portfolio features)

### Secondary Segments
- ARM holders (conversion opportunity)
- Jumbo loan holders (different rate dynamics)
- International expats with US properties
- First-time refinancers (education + guidance)

## Key Market Insights

### Insight 1: Automation Gap
No competitor offers truly automated, personalized refinance monitoring. Users either must:
- Check rates manually
- Receive generic alerts unrelated to their specific situation
- Get sold to constantly by lead gen sites

**Opportunity**: Own the "set it and forget it" category

### Insight 2: Trust Deficit
Lead generation business models have eroded consumer trust:
- 73% of homeowners distrust online mortgage comparison sites (J.D. Power)
- Users report feeling "sold" rather than helped

**Opportunity**: Subscription model aligns incentives with users

### Insight 3: Rate Cycle Timing
We may be entering a rate-cutting cycle (Fed pivot):
- Rates peaked at 7%+ in 2023
- Expected to decline over 2024-2026
- Creates optimal market entry window

**Opportunity**: Launch before the next refinance wave

### Insight 4: International Expansion
UK/Canada/Australia markets have:
- Shorter standard mortgage terms (2-5 years vs 30 in US)
- More frequent refinancing need
- Less competition in alert/monitoring space

**Opportunity**: International markets may have higher engagement
