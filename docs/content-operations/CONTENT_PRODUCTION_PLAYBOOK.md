# RefiAlert Content Production Playbook

A comprehensive guide for sustainable blog content production targeting ~10 posts per month.

---

## Publishing Schedule

**Frequency**: Every 3 days (~10 posts/month)

**Day-of-Week Rotation**:
- Week 1: Monday, Thursday
- Week 2: Sunday, Wednesday
- Week 3: Saturday, Tuesday
- Week 4: Friday, Monday

This rotation ensures varied day-of-week distribution for audience reach across different time zones.

**Optimal Publish Time**: 8:00 AM ET (peak engagement for financial content)

---

## Content Pipeline

### Phase 1: Planning (Week 1-2 of Month)

| Task | Owner | Timeline |
|------|-------|----------|
| Review editorial calendar | Content Lead | Day 1 |
| Research upcoming topics | Writer | Days 1-3 |
| Conduct keyword research | SEO Lead | Days 2-4 |
| Create post outlines | Writer | Days 4-7 |
| Source/create images | Designer | Days 5-7 |
| Approve outlines | Content Lead | Day 7 |

**Keyword Research Process**:
1. Start with primary topic from editorial calendar
2. Use tools: Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Ubersuggest
3. Identify primary keyword (search volume 500+, difficulty <60)
4. Find 3-5 secondary keywords
5. Check competitor content for that keyword
6. Document in content brief

### Phase 2: Writing (Week 3)

| Task | Owner | Timeline |
|------|-------|----------|
| Draft posts (batch 5-10) | Writer | Days 1-4 |
| Internal review | Content Lead | Day 5 |
| Author revisions | Writer | Days 6-7 |
| Final approval | Content Lead | Day 7 |

**Batch Writing Approach**:
- Write all posts for the month in one focused period
- Reduces context-switching
- Allows for consistent voice/style
- More efficient use of research time

### Phase 3: Production (Week 4)

| Task | Owner | Timeline |
|------|-------|----------|
| Upload to CMS | Content Lead | Days 1-2 |
| Add images & media | Designer | Days 1-2 |
| SEO optimization | SEO Lead | Days 2-3 |
| Internal linking | Content Lead | Days 3-4 |
| Schedule publishing | Content Lead | Day 4 |
| Queue social posts | Marketing | Days 4-5 |

## Production Models

### Option A: AI-Generated Content

**Process**:
1. Generate draft using GPT-4/Claude from outline
2. Human editor reviews for accuracy
3. Fact-check all statistics and claims
4. Edit for voice and style consistency
5. Final review before publishing

**Estimated Monthly Cost**: ~$50 (API costs + tools)

**Pros**:
- Fast turnaround
- Consistent output
- Low cost

**Cons**:
- Requires rigorous fact-checking
- May lack industry insights
- Needs heavy editing for authenticity

### Option B: Freelance Writers

**Process**:
1. Hire subject matter experts (mortgage/finance writers)
2. Provide detailed briefs and guidelines
3. Writer submits draft
4. Editor reviews and requests revisions
5. Final approval and publish

**Estimated Monthly Cost**: $500-1,000 (10 posts x $50-100/post)

**Where to Find Writers**:
- Contently, Skyword (premium)
- ProBlogger, BloggingPro (job boards)
- Finance-specific: ClearVoice
- Upwork (with careful vetting)

**Pros**:
- Industry expertise
- Original perspectives
- Less editing required

**Cons**:
- Higher cost
- Variable availability
- Onboarding time

### Option C: Hybrid Approach (Recommended)

**Process**:
1. AI generates research summary and outline
2. Human writer expands into full draft
3. AI assists with SEO optimization
4. Human editor finalizes

**Estimated Monthly Cost**: $200-300

**Workflow**:
```
Research (AI) -> Outline (AI) -> Draft (Human) -> SEO (AI) -> Edit (Human) -> Publish
```

**Pros**:
- Best of both worlds
- Cost-effective
- Maintains human quality
- Scales efficiently

## Performance Tracking

### Monthly Metrics Dashboard

Track these KPIs monthly:

| Metric | Target | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Organic traffic | +10% MoM | Google Analytics |
| Keyword rankings | Top 10 for 20% of posts | Google Search Console |
| Time on page | >3 minutes | Google Analytics |
| Bounce rate | <60% | Google Analytics |
| Email signups | 50/month from blog | SendGrid |
| Calculator usage | 100/month from blog | Internal analytics |

### Quarterly Content Audit

Every quarter, audit content performance:

1. **Identify top performers** (top 10% by traffic)
   - Analyze what made them successful
   - Create more similar content
   - Update with fresh data

2. **Identify underperformers** (bottom 20%)
   - Review for quality issues
   - Check keyword targeting
   - Consider consolidation or deletion

3. **Update statistics and data**
   - Refresh rate information
   - Update year references
   - Verify external links still work

## Content Refresh Strategy

### 6-Month Audit Cycle

| Month | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | Review posts 6+ months old |
| 2 | Update statistics/data |
| 3 | Refresh for algorithm changes |
| 4 | Add new internal links |
| 5 | Update meta descriptions |
| 6 | Full performance review |

### Refresh Triggers

Update content immediately when:
- Mortgage rates change significantly (>0.5% shift)
- Major regulatory changes occur
- New lending products launch
- Calculator features are updated
- Seasonal relevance (tax season, homebuying season)

### Refresh Checklist

For each content refresh:
- [ ] Update year in title if applicable
- [ ] Verify all statistics are current
- [ ] Check external links are working
- [ ] Add links to newer related content
- [ ] Update meta description if needed
- [ ] Re-submit to Google Search Console
- [ ] Update social sharing images if outdated

## Tools & Resources

### Content Creation
- **Grammarly** - Grammar and style checking
- **Hemingway Editor** - Readability scoring (target: 60+)

### SEO
- **Ahrefs/SEMrush** - Keyword research

### Images

- **Canva** - Custom graphics
- **TinyPNG** - Image compression (<100KB target)

### Scheduling
- **Buffer/Hootsuite** - Social media scheduling
- **CMS scheduling** - Post scheduling

## Emergency Procedures

### Rate Alert Content

When major rate changes occur:
1. Publish quick update post within 24 hours
2. Update all rate-related content within 48 hours
3. Send email notification to subscribers
4. Social media announcement

### Content Issues

**Factual Error Discovered**:
1. Immediately correct the error
2. Add correction note at top of post
3. Notify readers via email if significant

**Plagiarism Claim**:
1. Remove content immediately
2. Investigate claim thoroughly
3. Take appropriate action (rewrite or remove)
